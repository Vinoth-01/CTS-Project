FAQ's

1. What personal information is required when submitting a claim?
You will need to provide the patient’s full name, date of birth, treatment details, and insurance policy number.
2. Are there any guidelines on how to name the files when uploading them?
It is recommended to use descriptive names that include the patient’s name and type of document (e.g., "JohnDoe_MedicalReport.pdf") to help the insurance company easily identify each file.
3. What formats are accepted for uploading medical documents?
The platform accepts a variety of formats including PDF, JPEG, and PNG. It is recommended to use PDF for documents as it ensures that all pages are included in a single file.
4. What happens if there’s a discrepancy in the information provided?
If there is a discrepancy, the claim might be delayed or rejected. You will be notified of the issue and can correct the information directly contacting the agent.
5. How long does it take for the insurance company to process a claim?
The processing time can vary depending on the complexity of the claim. Typically, claims are processed within 7-14 business days.
6. What should I do if my claim is rejected?
If your claim is rejected, you will receive a notification explaining the reason. You can then correct any errors or provide additional information to resubmit your claim for reconsideration.
7. How secure is the medical data I upload on this platform?
The platform uses advanced encryption and security protocols to ensure that your data is protected at all times. Only authorized personnel from the hospital and insurance company can access the information.
8. What type of medical data do I need to upload for an insurance claim?
You need to upload essential documents such as the patient’s medical reports, lab results, discharge summaries, and any other documentation related to the treatment. Ensure all documents are clear and legible.
9. Can I save my progress and submit the claim later?
Unfortunately, the platform currently does not support saving progress.
